✦歷史的啟示Lessons from History✦

讀遍上百篇興衰起落，最值得帶走的，其實只有三句經過時間淬鍊的話。Across a hundred chronicles of rise and fall, only three truths — tempered by time — are truly worth carrying away.

I
時間的韻腳The Rhyme of Time
人性不變，所以歷史會押韻Human nature never changes — so history rhymes
「已有的事，後必再有；日光之下，並無新事。」——《傳道書》“What has been will be again; there is nothing new under the sun.” — Ecclesiastes
三千年來，技術翻天覆地，可是驅動人類的那兩股力量——貪婪與恐懼——從未改變。於是每一場泡沫、每一次擠兌、每一回盛世與崩盤，都似曾相識。讀歷史最大的用處，不是預測未來，而是學會在一再重演的劇本裡，認出那個重複犯錯的自己。For three thousand years technology has been turned upside down, yet the two forces that move us — greed and fear — have never changed. So every bubble, every bank run, every golden age and every crash feels strangely familiar. The deepest use of history is not to foretell the future, but to recognise, in a script played out again and again, the same erring self.
印證於Seen in貪婪與恐懼Greed & Fear·景氣循環The Business Cycle·賭性The Gamble
II
文明的地基The Foundation
真正的財富，是信任與制度，而非金銀Real wealth is trust and institutions, not gold
「民無信不立。」——《論語》“Without the people’s trust, a state cannot stand.” — Confucius
西班牙曾把整片新大陸的白銀，搬回自己的港口，卻依舊走向貧弱；荷蘭與英國沒有金山，卻靠著銀行、保險、契約與法治——一整套被具象化的「信任」——成了富強之邦。錢、銀行、保險、股票，說到底都是同一種東西：陌生人之間，願意彼此相信的那份承諾。它是文明看不見的地基，築起來要幾百年，崩塌只需一瞬。Spain hauled the silver of an entire New World back to its ports, and grew poor all the same; the Dutch and the British had no mountains of gold, yet on banks, insurance, contracts and the rule of law — trust made tangible — they built mighty, prosperous nations. Money, banks, insurance, shares: in the end all are the same thing — the promise strangers are willing to place in one another. It is the invisible foundation of civilisation: centuries to build, an instant to fall.
印證於Seen in信任·貨幣Money & Trust·銀行三千年Banks·保險Insurance
III
權力的無常The Impermanence of Power
盛極而衰，物極必反：沒有什麼大到不會倒At the zenith, decline begins — nothing is too big to fall
「禍兮福之所倚，福兮禍之所伏。」——《老子》“Disaster is what fortune leans upon; fortune is where disaster hides.” — Laozi
每一個帝國，都曾相信自己會是例外，會千秋萬代。可是輝煌的羅馬、王朝循環裡的中國、日不落的大英、看似大到不能倒的金融巨獸——衰敗的種子，往往正是在最強盛的那一刻，被「成功」親手種下：自滿、傲慢、僵化、過度擴張。歷史最謙卑的一課是：最危險的時刻，從來不是谷底，而是巔峰。Every empire once believed itself the exception, destined to last forever. Yet imperial Rome, the dynastic cycles of China, the empire on which the sun never set, the financial giants too big to fail — the seeds of their decline were usually planted, by success itself, at the very height of their power: complacency, hubris, rigidity, over-reach. History’s most humbling lesson: the most dangerous moment is never the bottom, but the summit.
印證於Seen in王朝循環China’s Dynastic Cycle·俄羅斯Russia·大到不能倒Too Big to Fail
