EXECUTIVE SUMMARY執行摘要

Busan is South Korea's second-largest city and principal container port (world's 7th-busiest at ~24M TEU annually), home to 3.4 million residents across its metropolitan area. MOLIT transaction data puts the Busan average apartment price per m² at ~₩7.5M/m² (median ₩6.8M/m²), which for a typical 85 m² unit implies ~₩640M (~US$435K), with weak YoY performance — a significantly more affordable entry point versus Seoul, where comparable units cost 3.4x more (and the gap has widened from 2.8x in 2019). Gross rental yields average 4.5%, materially above the BOK policy rate of 3.00%, providing positive carry for leveraged investors — a structural advantage Busan retains over Seoul where prime yields of 1.8% imply meaningful negative carry.

Three factors shape the 2026 investment thesis. First, Haeundae waterfront and Marine City command Seoul-adjacent pricing (₩680–850M) but benefit from tourist and festival demand unavailable to Seoul stock. Second, the North Port Redevelopment — an $8B, 2025–2035 project to transform 1.53M m² of aging port facilities into a mixed-use waterfront district with 15,000 residential units — is the largest urban-transformation catalyst in Korea since Yongsan IBD. Third, Busan faces demographic headwinds: median age 45+, net out-migration to Seoul, and the fastest-aging population among Korea's major cities — tempering long-term demand and creating a bifurcation between prime waterfront (supported) and outer suburbs (pressured).

釜山是韓國第二大城市及主要貨櫃港（全球第7繁忙，年吞吐約2,400萬TEU），都會區人口340萬。公寓均價約3.5億韓元（約$255K），年增+3.0%——相較首爾同類單位價格高3.4倍（2019年為2.8倍，差距擴大），釜山提供明顯更可負擔入場點。毛租金收益率平均4.5%，明顯高於BOK政策利率3.00%，為槓桿投資者提供正利差——釜山相對首爾的結構性優勢（首爾頂級收益率1.8%，意味著顯著負利差）。

三大因素塑造2026年投資命題。其一，海雲台濱海與Marine City享受近首爾定價（6.8-8.5億韓元），同時受惠於首爾存量無法取得的旅遊與影展需求。其二，北港再開發——80億美元、2025-2035年項目將153萬平方米老化港口設施改造為含15,000套住宅單位的混合用途濱海區——是繼龍山IBD後韓國最大的城市轉型催化劑。其三，釜山面臨人口逆風：中位年齡45歲以上、對首爾淨外流、韓國主要城市中人口老化最快——抑制長期需求並造成頂級濱海（受支撐）與外圍郊區（承壓）的分化。

PRICE TREND vs SEOUL房價走勢與首爾比較

Busan's apartment market has exhibited materially lower volatility than Seoul's over the 2019–2025 cycle. Prices rose +8% in 2021 (vs. Seoul's +12.5%) driven by spillover demand and ultra-low interest rates, corrected -4% in 2022 (vs. Seoul's -4.2%), and have since stabilized with 2024–2025 showing a gradual +3% recovery. The Seoul-to-Busan price gap has widened from 2.8x (2019) to 3.4x (2025), reflecting disproportionate capital concentration in the capital and Busan's weaker household-formation tailwind.

The market is dominated by resale (secondary) transactions — ~75% of volume — as new launches have been concentrated in Gimhae and outer satellite cities rather than the urban core. Peripheral satellite cities such as Gimhae (₩250M), Yangsan (₩220M), and Sasang (₩300M) offer more affordable alternatives at 60–85% of Busan core prices, particularly attractive to first-time buyers and retirees. Unlike Seoul, Busan does not operate under a "speculation over-heated district" designation, meaning LTV and DSR rules are the non-regulated tier: first-home LTV 70%, second-home 60% — materially looser than Seoul's 40%/30%.

釜山公寓市場2019-2025週期內波動性明顯低於首爾。2021年受外溢需求與超低利率推動上漲+8%（首爾+12.5%），2022年修正-4%（首爾-4.2%），此後穩定，2024-2025年逐步復甦+3%。首爾對釜山價差從2019年2.8倍擴大至2025年3.4倍，反映資本對首都的過度集中及釜山家庭形成順風較弱。

市場由二手（轉售）交易主導——佔成交約75%——因新推案集中於金海及外圍衛星城市而非城市核心。衛星城市如金海（2.5億韓元）、梁山（2.2億韓元）、沙上（3.0億韓元）以釜山核心價60-85%提供更可負擔替代，對首購族與退休人員尤具吸引力。與首爾不同，釜山不屬於「投機過熱區」指定，意味著LTV與DSR規則為非監管層：首購LTV 70%、第二套60%——明顯較首爾40%/30%寬鬆。

KEY DISTRICTS DEEP DIVE重點地區深度分析

Haeundae-gu (Marine City / Dongbaek / Jung-dong) is Busan's premier waterfront district, commanding the highest prices citywide. Marine City's 60+ story ocean-front luxury towers trade at ₩850M; standard Haeundae-beach stock at ₩680M; interior Haeundae at ₩520M. The district hosts the BIFF (Busan International Film Festival) every October, BEXCO convention center, and the highest concentration of international-school options (Busan International Foreign School, IKSC). Foreign buyer interest is highest here — Japanese, Taiwanese, and Chinese investors collectively represent ~15% of Marine City transactions.

Centum City (within Haeundae-gu) is the tech and commerce hub anchored by BEXCO and the Shinsegae Centum City department store (Guinness-certified as the world's largest). Nampo / Jung-gu is the historic old downtown around Jagalchi Fish Market, now gentrifying and directly adjacent to the North Port redevelopment zone — prices here have risen 12% over 2024–2025 in anticipation. Seomyeon / Busanjin-gu is the transportation spine (Line 1/2 interchange) with strong retail-led rental demand. Gimhae offers the most affordable metropolitan entry at ₩250M with airport proximity (Gimhae International) and light-rail connection — the fastest-growing satellite, attracting young families priced out of Haeundae.

海雲台區（Marine City / 冬柏 / 中洞）是釜山首屈一指的濱海區，全市最高價。Marine City的60層以上海景豪華塔樓8.5億韓元；海雲台標準海灘存量6.8億韓元；海雲台內陸5.2億韓元。該區每年10月舉辦BIFF（釜山國際電影節）、BEXCO會展中心、最密集國際學校選擇（釜山國際外國人學校、IKSC）。外資買家興趣在此最高——日、台、中投資者合計佔Marine City交易約15%。

Centum City（海雲台區內）是以BEXCO與新世界Centum City百貨（金氏世界紀錄認證世界最大）為錨的科技商業樞紐。南浦/中區為扎嘎其魚市周邊歷史舊城，目前都市更新且直接鄰接北港再開發區——此處價格2024-2025年因預期上漲12%。西面/釜山鎮區為交通主幹（1/2號線交會），零售導向租賃需求強。金海以2.5億韓元提供最可負擔都會入場價，鄰近機場（金海國際）與輕軌連接——成長最快的衛星城，吸引被海雲台擠出的年輕家庭。

NORTH PORT REDEVELOPMENT北港再開發

The Busan North Port Redevelopment (부산북항재개발) is the city's most transformative urban initiative in a generation. Managed by the Busan Port Authority (BPA) with private sector partners, it transforms 1.53 million m² of aging port facilities into a waterfront mixed-use district. Total investment: $8 billion ($8.2B by current FX) over 2025–2035. Key components: (i) 15,000 residential units across high-rise and mid-rise towers, (ii) 1.2M m² of commercial office and retail, (iii) an opera house and cultural facilities, (iv) a new cruise-ship terminal expanding capacity 3x, (v) 270K m² of parkland, and (vi) a Marine Leisure Park. The project is comparable in scope to London's Docklands (Canary Wharf era) or Sydney's Darling Harbour.

Strategic impact: the project shifts Busan's center of gravity, physically reconnecting Jung-gu old downtown (currently isolated by the port) with the Haeundae corridor via a new 1.5km waterfront promenade. Phased delivery: Phase 1 infrastructure (2026–2028), residential unit delivery (2029–2033). Land parcels adjacent to the zone — particularly in Jung-gu and Yeongdo-gu — have already seen 8–15% price appreciation since the revised master plan approval in early 2024. Early-mover risk: construction delays on Korean mega-projects (Yongsan IBD slipped 15 years) suggest a probability-weighted timeline closer to 2038 for full completion.

釜山北港再開發（부산북항재개발）是該城一代最具變革性的城市計劃。由釜山港灣公社（BPA）與民間夥伴管理，將153萬平方米老化港口設施改造為濱海混合用途區。總投資80億美元（以當前匯率82億），2025-2035年分期。主要組成：(i) 15,000套住宅單位（高層與中層塔樓）；(ii) 120萬平方米商辦與零售；(iii) 歌劇院與文化設施；(iv) 新郵輪碼頭容量擴3倍；(v) 27萬平方米公園綠地；(vi) 海上休閒公園。規模可比倫敦碼頭區（金絲雀碼頭時代）或雪梨達令港。

戰略影響：該項目轉移釜山重心，以新建1.5公里濱海步道實體連接中區舊城（目前被港口隔絕）與海雲台走廊。分階段交付：第一階段基礎設施（2026-2028），住宅單位交付（2029-2033）。鄰近區地塊——尤其中區與影島區——自2024年初修訂總規批覆以來已出現8-15%價格升值。早期風險：韓國大型項目施工延誤（龍山IBD延誤15年）暗示概率加權時間軸接近2038年全面完工。

TOURISM & MICE DEMAND觀光與會展需求

Busan attracts 13M+ tourists annually in 2025, surpassing pre-COVID levels, with Japanese (~4.2M), Chinese (~2.8M), Taiwanese (~1.1M), and SEA visitors dominant. The Busan International Film Festival (BIFF), held every October, drives global visibility and is Asia's largest film festival. BEXCO hosts 200+ MICE events annually including G-Star gaming convention, Korea Marine Equipment Show, and ITU Telecom. While the 2030 World Expo bid was lost to Riyadh in Nov 2023, the infrastructure investment pipeline (expanded airport, upgraded KTX, new cruise terminal) is proceeding regardless.

The tourism-rental nexus is strongest in Haeundae, where short-term rental demand during summer (Jun–Aug) and festival season (BIFF in October) can push premium-unit occupancy to 70%+ annually at rates 30–40% above standard long-term rents. However, the regulatory framework for short-term rentals mirrors Seoul's: the Hanok Tourism Act and Tourism Promotion Act define permissible structures, but condo-based short-term rental requires "urban living housing" registration — a high bar. Most Airbnb-style operations are in grey-zone compliance. Investors modeling STR returns should apply a 30–40% discount for compliance and occupancy-risk adjustment.

釜山2025年吸引超過1,300萬遊客，超越疫前水平，日本（約420萬）、中國（約280萬）、台灣（約110萬）、東南亞訪客主導。每年10月舉辦的釜山國際電影節（BIFF）驅動全球知名度，為亞洲最大電影節。BEXCO年舉辦200多場MICE活動，含G-Star遊戲展、韓國海事設備展、ITU Telecom。雖2030世博申辦於2023年11月敗給利雅德，基礎設施投資管線（擴建機場、升級KTX、新郵輪碼頭）仍推進。

旅遊-租賃連結在海雲台最強，夏季（6-8月）與影展季（10月BIFF）短租需求可將優質單位年入住率推至70%+、租金高於標準長租30-40%。然而短租監管框架與首爾相同：韓屋旅遊法與旅遊促進法定義允許結構，但公寓基礎短租需「城市生活住宅」登記——高門檻。多數Airbnb式運營處於灰色地帶。建模STR回報的投資者應套用30-40%折扣以調整合規與入住風險。

RENTAL MARKET & YIELD租賃市場與收益

Busan offers gross rental yields of 4.5% on average — among the best in South Korea and materially above Seoul's 2.5%. Haeundae 2-bedroom apartments rent for ₩1.2–1.8M/month ($870–$1,310), with Marine City premium units commanding ₩2.5M/month. The jeonse (lump-sum deposit) ratio in Busan remains 62% — higher than Seoul's 58% — reflecting the older tenant demographic and slower wolse-conversion pace. Monthly rental (wolse) is growing 6–8% p.a. Occupancy rates in Haeundae exceed 92%.

Tenant composition differs materially from Seoul. Japanese corporate expatriates (~5,000 in Busan metro, concentrated in Centum City near fintech/broadcasting, and Haeundae near international schools) are the largest foreign cohort — driven by Japanese-owned ports, logistics, and Renesas/Murata semiconductor suppliers. Chinese students at Busan's 20+ universities create predictable wolse demand near Sasang and Pusan National University campus. Retiree segment is material: Busan is a top-3 domestic retirement destination, tempering the urban-exit dynamic despite aging demographics. For foreign investors, wolse structures are standard since jeonse requires Korean banking access and tax-residency documentation.

釜山毛租金收益率平均4.5%——韓國最佳之一，明顯高於首爾2.5%。海雲台兩房公寓月租120-180萬韓元（$870-$1,310），Marine City優質單位達250萬韓元/月。釜山全租（一次性保證金）比率維持62%——高於首爾58%——反映較年長租客人口與較慢的月租轉換步調。月租（wolse）年增6-8%。海雲台入住率超過92%。

租客構成與首爾明顯不同。日本企業外派（釜山都會區約5,000人，集中Centum City鄰近金融科技/廣播、海雲台鄰近國際學校）為最大外籍群體——由日資港口、物流及Renesas/Murata半導體供應商驅動。中國學生在釜山20多所大學創造沙上與釜山大學校園附近穩定月租需求。退休族群重要：釜山為國內前三大退休目的地，儘管老齡化抑制都市外流動態。對外資投資者，月租結構為標準，因全租需韓國銀行管道與稅務居留文件。

DEMOGRAPHICS & NET MIGRATION人口結構與淨遷移

Busan's demographic profile is the single biggest long-term risk to the property thesis. The city has lost population for 10+ consecutive years — from 3.56M (2010) to 3.40M (2025) — while the national population held flat, implying net out-migration of ~8,000 people per year. Outflow is concentrated in the 20–39 age cohort relocating to Seoul and Gyeonggi Province for higher-wage employment. Busan's median age is 45.6 years (vs. Seoul's 43.3, national 44.2), and the fertility rate at 0.66 is below the national 0.72 — the lowest among Korea's six major metro cities.

Counterforces mitigating but not fully offsetting the demographic pressure: (i) domestic retiree inflow — Busan consistently ranks top-3 Korean retirement destinations due to climate, healthcare quality, and coastal amenity; (ii) Japanese expatriate community growth from port/logistics and Japan-friendly culture; (iii) North Port redevelopment is expected to reverse some out-migration when jobs materialize post-2028. The net implication for property: prime waterfront (Haeundae) remains supported by foreign tenant demand, retirees, and tourism; outer suburbs (Saha, Buk-gu, Gangseo-gu) face structural pressure. Investors should concentrate in the core rather than spread risk across satellites.

釜山人口結構特徵是房產命題的最大單一長期風險。該城已連續10多年流失人口——從2010年356萬降至2025年340萬——同時全國人口持平，意味著每年淨外流約8,000人。外流集中於20-39歲群體，遷往首爾與京畿道追求高薪就業。釜山中位年齡45.6歲（首爾43.3、全國44.2），生育率0.66低於全國0.72——韓國六大都會區中最低。

緩解但未完全抵消人口壓力的反作用力：(i) 國內退休人員流入——釜山因氣候、醫療品質、沿海設施持續名列韓國前三退休目的地；(ii) 日本外派社區增長，來自港口/物流與日本友善文化；(iii) 北港再開發預期2028年後就業實現時扭轉部分外流。對房產的淨意涵：頂級濱海（海雲台）因外籍租戶需求、退休人員、觀光仍受支撐；外圍郊區（沙下、北區、江西區）面臨結構性壓力。投資者應集中於核心而非分散於衛星城市。

OUTLOOK & SCENARIOS展望與情景

Base case (+2–4%/yr, probability 55%): Steady prime waterfront appreciation supported by North Port Phase 1 progress (infrastructure 2026–2028), tourism continuing to grow 8–10%, and BOK rate cuts. Haeundae reaches ₩740M by 2028. Non-prime districts flat-to-slightly-up; Gimhae/Yangsan satellites outperform on affordability. Yield stays 4.5% average. Bull case (+5–7%/yr, probability 25%): Faster BOK rate cuts to 2.00%. Japanese tourist surge on continued yen weakness (USD/JPY 160+). North Port anchor tenant announcements accelerate residential unit presales. Haeundae reaches ₩820M by 2028. Foreign-buyer share rises to 20%+ of Marine City transactions.

Bear case (-1 to +1%/yr, probability 20%): Population outflow to Seoul accelerates as the age-cohort imbalance compounds. Net migration worsens to -12K/year. North Port Phase 1 slips to 2030+. BOK maintains tight policy on USD strength. Haeundae stalls at ₩680M; non-prime districts decline 3–5%.
