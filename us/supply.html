INVENTORY LEVELS庫存水準

The U.S. housing inventory crisis remains fundamentally structural. Despite a modest recovery in national active listings from 2024 lows, the majority of monitored metros remain far below the 6.0 months threshold that defines a balanced market. The most constrained markets are concentrated in tech-driven coastal metros: Silicon Valley sits at just 0.8 months of supply, San Francisco at 0.8 months (SF homes only), and Chicago city proper at 0.72 months — all effectively representing sub-30-day theoretical clearing times. Even traditionally well-supplied markets like Boston have tightened to 1.5–2.0 months.

The least constrained markets tell an equally important story. Miami condos have surged to 14.0 months of supply — deep into buyer’s market territory — driven by a glut of speculative condo construction and rising insurance costs. Austin has reached 6.1 months, finally crossing the balanced threshold after years of overbuilding. Denver’s metro carries 17,107 active listings, representing one of the highest inventory-to-sales ratios among Sun Belt metros.

美國住宅庫存危機本質上仍屬結構性問題。儘管全國活躍掛牌量從2024年低點溫和復甦，大多數受監測的都會區仍遠低於定義均衡市場的6.0個月門檻。最受約束的市場集中在科技驅動的沿海都會區：矽谷僅有0.8個月供給，舊金山0.8個月（僅獨棟），芝加哥市區0.72個月——均實質代表不到30天的理論出清時間。即使是傳統供給充足的市場如波士頓也已收緊至1.5至2.0個月。

供給最寬鬆的市場同樣值得關注。邁阿密公寓已飆升至14.0個月供給——深入買方市場——由投機性公寓建設過剩和保險成本上升推動。奧斯丁已達6.1個月，在多年過度建設後終於越過均衡門檻。丹佛都會區攜帶17,107套活躍掛牌，代表陽光帶都會區中最高的庫存銷售比之一。

BUILDING PERMITS建築許可

Building permit issuance across the 12-city monitor has declined meaningfully from the 2021–2022 pandemic boom peaks. Higher interest rates, elevated construction costs, and tightening credit conditions have combined to slow the pipeline. The national single-family permit count fell ~12% from the 2022 peak, while multifamily starts have dropped even more sharply — down ~35% from the 2022 high as developers face refinancing walls and reduced institutional appetite.

The decline is uneven. Austin, which led the nation in per-capita permitting during 2021–2022, has pulled back ~28% from its peak indexed level as the local market digests oversupply. Phoenix similarly retreated from its 2022 highs. In contrast, Boston and Chicago — markets with historically lower permit volumes — have seen permits stabilise closer to pre-pandemic levels rather than experiencing dramatic swings, as these metros never participated in the Sun Belt building boom.

12城市監測中的建築許可發放量已從2021至2022年疫情繁榮峰值顯著下降。更高的利率、升高的建設成本和收緊的信貸條件共同減緩了管道。全國獨棟建築許可數量從2022年峰值下降約12%，多戶住宅開工更急劇下降——從2022年高點下降約35%，因開發商面臨再融資牆和機構投資意願降低。

下降幅度不均。奧斯丁在2021至2022年期間人均許可量領先全國，但已從峰值指數水準回撤約28%，因本地市場消化供給過剩。鳳凰城同樣從2022年高點回落。相比之下，波士頓和芝加哥——歷來許可量較低的市場——許可量穩定在接近疫情前水準而非經歷劇烈波動，因這些都會區從未參與陽光帶建設潮。

LOCK-IN EFFECT利率鎖定效應

The mortgage rate lock-in effect remains the single most powerful force suppressing existing-home supply. With an estimated ~60% of outstanding U.S. mortgages carrying rates below 4%, and the current 30-year fixed rate at 6.38%, homeowners face a punishing monthly payment penalty for selling and repurchasing at today’s rates. This dynamic has effectively removed an estimated ~33% of normal annual resale supply from the market — roughly 1.5 million transactions that would have occurred under stable-rate conditions.

The penalty is starkest for borrowers who locked in at 3.0% during the 2020–2021 trough. On a $400,000 mortgage, upgrading from 3.0% to 6.38% adds $1,729/month in payment — a de facto cost of mobility that freezes households in place. Even a more moderate scenario of moving from 3.0% to 5.5% imposes $1,343/month in additional cost. Until rates fall meaningfully below 5%, the lock-in effect will continue to distort supply across all 12 monitored cities.

抵押貸款利率鎖定效應仍是抑制現有住宅供給的最強大單一力量。估計約60%的美國現有抵押貸款利率低於4%，而當前30年固定利率為6.38%，屋主在以今日利率出售並重新購買時面臨懲罰性的月供增加。這一動態已有效從市場移除約33%的正常年度轉售供給——大約150萬筆在利率穩定條件下本會發生的交易。

對於在2020至2021年低谷期間鎖定3.0%利率的借款人，懲罰最為嚴重。以40萬美元抵押貸款計，從3.0%升至6.38%每月增加1,729美元——實質上是凍結家庭不動的流動性成本。即使是從3.0%升至5.5%的較溫和情境也增加1,343美元月供。在利率顯著降至5%以下之前，鎖定效應將繼續扭曲所有12個受監測城市的供給。

SUPPLY CLIFFS供給斷崖

Several markets face impending supply cliffs as the multifamily construction pipeline — initiated during the 2021–2022 boom — delivers its final wave and new starts fail to replace it. Boston is the most vulnerable: the metro’s multifamily pipeline peaked in 2023 with approximately 18,000 units under construction, but new permit issuance has dropped to levels that imply a ~45% decline in deliveries by 2027.

Denver faces a similar trajectory. The metro’s apartment completion rate peaked in 2024 at approximately 14,500 units, but the pipeline thins dramatically into 2026–2027 as developers pulled back amid rising cap rates and falling rents. Austin’s cliff is the most immediate: after delivering a record ~28,000 multifamily units in 2023–2024, new starts have collapsed and 2027 deliveries are projected at less than half the peak. These supply cliffs will eventually tighten rental markets and, by extension, redirect demand pressure back toward the for-sale market.

數個市場面臨即將到來的供給斷崖，因為在2021至2022年繁榮期啟動的多戶住宅建設在建量交付最後一波，而新開工未能替代。波士頓最脆弱：該都會區多戶住宅管道在2023年達到峰值，約有18,000套在建，但新許可發放量已降至暗示2027年交付量將下降約45%的水準。

丹佛面臨相似軌跡。該都會區公寓竣工率在2024年達到約14,500套的峰值，但管道在2026至2027年急劇萎縮，因開發商在資本化率上升和租金下降中收縮。奧斯丁的斷崖最為迫切：在2023至2024年交付創紀錄的約28,000套多戶住宅後，新開工已崩潰，2027年交付預計不到峰值的一半。這些供給斷崖最終將收緊租賃市場，並由此將需求壓力重新導向待售市場。
