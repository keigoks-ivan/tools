EXECUTIVE SUMMARY執行摘要

Phoenix's housing market in 2026 represents the textbook case study of the American sunbelt pandemic cycle: extreme appreciation driven by migration and cheap money, followed by correction as both tailwinds reversed, now settling into a multi-speed recovery defined by neighborhood-level divergence. The Case-Shiller Phoenix index (PHXRSA) reached a peak of 343 in June 2022 — a 111% increase from January 2015 — before correcting to 330 by February 2025, a peak-to-trough decline of 3.8% on the CS metric. Phoenix median home price (city proper) stood at $444,000 in February 2026, down 1.33% YoY. Scottsdale, 15 miles northeast, posted a median of $1.0M in December 2025, up 15% YoY. These two figures from the same metro illustrate the defining market dynamic: Phoenix 2026 is not one market — it is a portfolio of diverging submarkets.

The correction from the 2022 peak has been orderly rather than distressed. Arizona inventory reached 27,000+ active statewide listings in May 2025 — a level not seen in nearly a decade — but this reflects normalization from the extreme lows of 2021 rather than distress-driven supply. Approximately 50% of Arizona homeowners hold more than $250,000 in equity — a buffer against any demand-driven price correction. The market has moved to approximately 4.4 months of supply — balanced market territory.

The structural risk unique to Phoenix is water. Arizona obtains approximately 41% of its water supply from groundwater pumping, and groundwater levels have been declining for decades. A 2023 state review found that several new subdivisions had insufficient groundwater for 100-year assured supply — triggering the state to halt some new home construction permits. This does not threaten near-term prices, but it fundamentally caps the decades-long growth trajectory that has characterized Phoenix since the 1980s.

2026年鳳凰城住宅市場是美國陽光地帶疫情週期的教科書案例研究。Case-Shiller鳳凰城指數（PHXRSA）在2022年6月達到343的峰值——自2015年1月以來漲幅111%——然後修正至2025年2月的330，峰值至谷值下跌3.8%。2026年2月鳳凰城市房屋中位數為444,000美元，年降1.33%。史考茲代爾在2025年12月錄得100萬美元的中位數，年增15%。來自同一都會區的這兩個數字說明了決定性的市場動態：2026年的鳳凰城不是一個市場——而是分化的子市場組合。

從2022年峰值的修正是有序而非困境驅動的。亞利桑那州庫存在2025年5月達到27,000套以上全州活躍掛牌——近十年未見的水平。約50%的亞利桑那州業主持有超過250,000美元的淨值——抵禦任何需求驅動的價格修正的緩衝。市場已移至約4.4個月供給——均衡市場範圍。

鳳凰城獨有的結構性風險是水資源。亞利桑那州約41%的水源來自地下水抽取，地下水位已下降數十年。2023年一項州級審查發現部分新住宅項目的地下水不足以保證100年確定供給——觸發州政府暫停部分新建房屋許可。這不會威脅近期價格，但從根本上限制了自1980年代以來鳳凰城特有的數十年增長軌跡。

PRICE TREND: PANDEMIC PEAK & CORRECTION房價走勢：疫情峰值與修正

Phoenix's price trajectory from 2015 to 2025 is one of the most extreme in modern U.S. real estate history. The CS Phoenix index rose from approximately 155 in January 2015 to 343 at the June 2022 peak — a 121% increase in 7.5 years, making Phoenix the single largest appreciator in the CS 20-city composite. The pandemic phase (2020–2022) alone saw the index rise 53% — the fastest two-year appreciation in Phoenix history.

The correction has been significant but contained. From the June 2022 peak of 343, the CS index declined approximately 12–13% to a trough of approximately 295–300 in early 2023, before recovering to 330 by February 2025. City-level median data tells a similar story: from a pandemic peak of approximately $453,000 (2022), the Phoenix city median declined to approximately $425,000 (2023 trough) before partially recovering to $444,000–$450,000 by late 2025. For a buy-and-hold buyer who entered in 2019, Phoenix remains a strong appreciation story; for a buyer who purchased at the 2022 peak, the real (inflation-adjusted) return through 2026 is modestly negative.

鳳凰城2015至2025年的房價走勢是現代美國房地產史上最極端的之一。CS鳳凰城指數從2015年1月的約155升至2022年6月峰值的343——7.5年漲幅121%，使鳳凰城在此期間成為CS 20城複合指數中單一漲幅最大的城市。疫情階段（2020至2022年）本身指數上漲53%——鳳凰城歷史上最快的兩年升值。

修正重大但可控。從2022年6月343的峰值，CS指數下跌約12至13%至2023年初約295至300的谷底，然後到2025年2月恢復至330。城市層面中位數數據呈現類似故事：從約453,000美元的疫情峰值（2022年），鳳凰城市中位數降至約425,000美元（2023年谷底），然後部分回升至2025年底的444,000至450,000美元。對於2019年入市的持有型買家，鳳凰城仍是強勁的升值故事；對於在2022年峰值購買的買家，到2026年的實際回報為小幅負值。

THE CORRECTION: INVESTOR RETREAT & NORMALIZATION修正解析：投資者撤退與正常化

Phoenix's correction from the 2022 peak has a distinct causal structure. The primary driver was investor retreat — the unwinding of pandemic-era speculative buying by individual investors, iBuyers (Opendoor, Offerpad, Zillow Offers), and small-scale landlords. When rates rose and appreciation expectations reversed, this cohort faced simultaneous pressure: high acquisition costs, elevated carrying costs, and falling rental yields. The result was a supply surge that took Arizona active listings from pandemic-era lows of approximately 6,000 to 27,000+ by May 2025.

The foreclosure picture tells the correction story cleanly. Maricopa County trustee deed filings totaled 317 in Q3 2025 — up 33% from Q2 but representing approximately 0.03% of total housing stock. During the 2009 GFC, Maricopa County had over 10,000 homes in pre-foreclosure. Most current distress is investor-owned: homeowners with sub-4% mortgages have almost no incentive to sell. The correction is an investor correction, not a homeowner correction — a structurally different and more benign phenomenon.

鳳凰城從2022年峰值的修正有明顯不同的因果結構。主要驅動力是投資者撤退——個人投資者、iBuyer和小規模房東的疫情時期投機性買入的平倉。當利率上升且升值預期逆轉時，供給激增使亞利桑那州活躍掛牌從約6,000套的低位升至2025年5月的27,000套以上。

止贖圖景清楚說明了修正故事。馬里科帕縣授託書文件在2025年第三季共317份——較第二季增長33%，但代表總住宅存量的約0.03%。在2009年全球金融危機期間，馬里科帕縣有超過10,000套房屋處於止贖前期。大多數當前困境是投資者持有。修正是投資者修正，而非業主修正——一個結構上更良性的現象。

WATER SCARCITY: THE LONG-TERM STRUCTURAL RISK水資源稀缺：長期結構性風險

Water is the defining long-term structural constraint on Phoenix's housing market. Arizona's water supply comes from three primary sources: the Colorado River (via the Central Arizona Project), groundwater aquifers, and smaller in-state surface water sources. Approximately 41% of Arizona's total water supply comes from groundwater pumping, and groundwater levels have been declining for decades as extraction rates exceed natural recharge rates.

In 2023, the Arizona Department of Water Resources (ADWR) determined that the Phoenix Active Management Area does not have sufficient assured groundwater for all planned future development. This triggered a halt on approvals for new subdivisions relying solely on groundwater in specific areas, including Rio Verde Highlands. The housing market implication is long-term rather than immediate: Phoenix can continue building for at least 10–15 years within existing allocations. But the 100-year assured supply constraint introduces a ceiling on the city's unlimited-growth narrative. Properties in areas with demonstrable long-term water security will command an increasing premium over those relying on groundwater or private wells.

水資源是決定鳳凰城住宅市場長期結構性約束的因素。亞利桑那州約41%的總水供應來自地下水抽取，地下水位已下降數十年。

2023年，亞利桑那州水資源部（ADWR）確定鳳凰城積極管理區域沒有足夠的確定地下水供應所有規劃的未來開發。這觸發了對包括Rio Verde Highlands在內特定地區審批的暫停。住宅市場影響是長期而非立即的：鳳凰城在現有分配範圍內至少可以繼續建設10至15年。但百年確定供給約束為城市的無限增長敘事引入了上限。具有可證明長期水資源安全性的區域房產將獲得越來越高的溢價。

NEIGHBORHOOD DIVERGENCE: SCOTTSDALE VS MARICOPA社區分化：史考茲代爾 VS 馬里科帕

The Phoenix metro's defining investment characteristic in 2026 is extreme neighborhood-level divergence. At the premium end, Scottsdale posted a December 2025 median of $1.0M (+15% YoY), driven by persistent demand from wealth-management professionals and retirees from high-cost coastal markets. Paradise Valley (median $4M+) operates in an effectively separate market. At the affordable end, Maricopa posted a December 2025 median of $335,000 (-7% YoY), reflecting the correction of pandemic-era exurban migration demand.

2026年鳳凰城都會區的決定性投資特徵是極端的社區層面分化。在高端市場，史考茲代爾在2025年12月錄得100萬美元的中位數（年增15%）。天堂谷（中位數400萬美元以上）在實際上獨立運作。在可負擔端，馬里科帕錄得335,000美元的中位數（年降7%），反映了疫情時代遠郊遷移需求的修正。

RENT CONTROL: ARIZONA’S TOTAL PREEMPTION租管：亞州完全先占

Arizona is a rent control preemption state. State statute (A.R.S. § 33-1329) explicitly prohibits any city, town, or other governmental unit from enacting, maintaining, or enforcing any ordinance that would control the amount of rent charged for private residential property. This is an absolute preemption with no exceptions.

Phoenix's rental market in 2025 saw the median rent at approximately $2,100/month (city), with Scottsdale at $2,800/month. Year-over-year rent growth softened to approximately 6.0%. Phoenix ranked as the fastest-declining rent market of any major U.S. metro in 2024, with median asking rents down approximately 8% from peak. The absence of rent control means the full rental market adjustment — both up and down — passes through directly to investors.

亞利桑那州是租管先占州。州法律（A.R.S. § 33-1329）明確禁止任何城市頒布或執行任何控制私人住宅房產租金的條例。這是無例外的絕對先占。

2025年鳳凰城租賃市場中位數租金約為2,100美元/月（城市），史考茲代爾為2,800美元/月。年度租金增長放緩至約6.0%。鳳凰城在2024年成為全美主要大都市中租金降速最快的市場，中位數要求租金較峰值下降約8%。租管缺失意味著完整的租賃市場調整直接傳導給投資者。

OUTLOOK 2026–2028展望 2026–2028

Phoenix's 2026–2028 outlook is the most internally divergent of any market in this monitor. The base case (55% probability) projects overall CS Phoenix appreciation of 2–4% annually through 2027, driven by continued net in-migration (Arizona job growth projected at 2.1–2.5%) and the resolution of the investor supply overhang. The CS index advances from 330 toward 345–355 by end-2027. Premium submarkets continue to appreciate 5–8%; affordable outer suburbs face continued flat-to-slightly-negative conditions.

The bull case (20% probability) requires meaningful rate decline below 5.5% and accelerated semiconductor investment. TSMC's $40 billion investment in two advanced chip fabrication facilities in North Phoenix is creating direct manufacturing employment (~6,000 workers at full ramp) and a large multiplier effect. In this scenario, metro-wide appreciation could reach 6–8% annually.

The bear case (25% probability) is dominated by water risk. A federal ruling reducing Arizona's Colorado River allocation combined with a drought year could trigger more widespread permit moratoriums and investor confidence collapse. Areas most dependent on groundwater face meaningful price declines (15–20%). The overall CS index could fall to 295–305, returning to 2023 trough levels. The bear case is geography-specific — premium water-secure areas would be far less affected than peripheral groundwater-dependent suburbs.

鳳凰城2026至2028年的展望是本監測任何市場中內部分化最大的。基本情境（概率55%）預計CS鳳凰城整體每年升值2至4%，CS指數推進至2027年底的345至355。優質子市場繼續在5至8%範圍內升值；可負擔外圍郊區面臨持續的持平至小幅負值條件。

樂觀情境（概率20%）需要利率下降至5.5%以下和半導體投資加速。台積電在北鳳凰城的400億美元投資正在創造直接製造業就業。在此情境下，都會區整體升值可達每年6至8%。

悲觀情境（概率25%）以水資源風險為主導。聯邦裁決削減亞利桑那州科羅拉多河分配加上乾旱年份，可能觸發更廣泛的許可暫停。最依賴地下水的地區面臨15至20%的有意義價格下跌。CS指數可能降至295至305，回到2023年谷底水平。悲觀情境具有地理特殊性。
