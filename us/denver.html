EXECUTIVE SUMMARY執行摘要

Denver's housing market in 2026 occupies an ambiguous position among the sunbelt correctors: more corrected than Phoenix (on a median price basis), less corrected than Austin (on a peak-to-trough basis), and facing a more complex set of headwinds than either. The Case-Shiller Denver index (DNXRSA) peaked at 325 in May 2022 and has declined only modestly to approximately 322 by February 2025, a peak-to-trough of less than 2%. The city of Denver median sale price was $538,950 in January 2026, down 1.11% YoY, with homes sitting on market for 81 days. The DMAR 2026 Economic Summit consensus: metro median will remain approximately $585,000 through 2026, representing flat-to-minimal appreciation.

The disconnect between the shallow CS decline and the buyer experience is explained by inventory. Active listings reached 17,107 in late 2025 — the most in over a decade, 50%+ above pre-pandemic levels. Denver ranks second nationally for delistings. Approximately 41.9% of Denver County listings have seen price cuts. The market has definitively shifted from seller-dominated to buyer-favorable conditions.

The most structurally significant headwind is Colorado's insurance crisis. The average homeowner insurance premium reached approximately $4,100/year by 2025 — a 137% increase over the prior decade, driven by the state's uniquely severe hail and wildfire risk profile. For condos, HOA fee surges have materially impaired buyer qualifications, contributing to condo sales falling 8% YoY and condo median prices declining 3.7% to $395,000.

2026年丹佛住宅市場在陽光地帶修正城市中處於模糊位置。Case-Shiller丹佛指數（DNXRSA）在2022年5月達到325的峰值，到2025年2月僅小幅下跌至約322，峰值至谷值不足2%。丹佛市2026年1月中位數成交價為538,950美元，年降1.11%，在市天數81天。DMAR 2026年經濟峰會共識：都會區中位數將全年維持在約585,000美元。

CS淺度下跌與買家實地體驗之間的脫節由庫存解釋。活躍掛牌在2025年底達到17,107套——十年多以來最多，比疫情前水平高出50%以上。丹佛在全國撤牌排名第二。約41.9%的丹佛縣掛牌已進行價格削減。

最具結構性意義的逆風是科羅拉多州的保險危機。平均房主保險費達到約4,100美元/年——過去十年漲幅137%，由州的獨特嚴峻冰雹和野火風險特徵驅動。對公寓而言，HOA費用激增實質損害了買家資格，使公寓銷量年降8%，中位數跌幅3.7%至395,000美元。

PRICE TREND: SHALLOW CS DECLINE, MEDIAN STILL HOLDING房價走勢：CS淺度下跌，中位數仍穩

Denver's price history is the quietest of the four sunbelt correctors. The CS Denver index rose from approximately 200 in January 2015 to 325 at the May 2022 peak — a 63% increase over 7.5 years, well below Phoenix's 121% or Austin's 116%. The pandemic surge was the most dramatic phase: the index rose 51% from January 2020 to May 2022. The correction from peak has been almost invisible on the CS metric (−2%).

The Denver metro SF median of approximately $625,000 (2025) represents roughly flat appreciation since 2022 in nominal terms. The city of Denver median was $583,000 in late 2025 before seasonal softening to $539,000 in January 2026. The 10-year appreciation (2015 to 2025) has been approximately 75% — strong in absolute terms but below Phoenix, Miami, and Austin. Denver's appreciation story is solid rather than spectacular, mapping to a diversified economic base across aerospace, energy, healthcare, and a growing tech cluster.

丹佛的房價歷史是四個陽光地帶修正城市中最平靜的。CS丹佛指數從2015年1月的約200升至2022年5月峰值的325——7.5年漲幅63%，遠低於鳳凰城的121%或奧斯丁的116%。疫情漲幅是最戲劇性的階段：指數上漲51%。從峰值的修正在CS指標上幾乎無形（-2%）。

丹佛都會區獨棟住宅約625,000美元的中位數（2025年）在名義上自2022年以來幾乎持平。丹佛市中位數在2025年底為約583,000美元，季節性軟化至2026年1月的539,000美元。10年升值約75%——在絕對意義上強勁，但低於鳳凰城、邁阿密和奧斯丁。丹佛的升值故事穩健而非壯觀，映射了跨航空航天、能源、醫療和不斷增長的科技群聚的多元化經濟基礎。

THE CORRECTION: INVENTORY SURGE & BUYER’S MARKET ARRIVAL修正：庫存激增與買方市場到來

Denver's correction manifests primarily through inventory expansion and time-on-market extension rather than dramatic price declines. Active listings reached 17,107 in December 2025 — the highest in more than a decade, 50%+ above pre-pandemic norms. Denver ranks second nationally for delistings. Approximately 41.9% of Denver County listings have had price reductions. The sale-to-list ratio has softened to below 98%.

The buyer's market arrival is evidenced by: 81 days on market (city, January 2026) versus 10–15 days at the 2021 peak; November 2025 closed sales down 23% MoM; active listings up 8.64% YoY in January 2026. The Colorado Association of REALTORS® characterizes the current environment as “recalibration rather than downturn.”

丹佛的修正主要通過庫存擴張和在市時間延長顯現。活躍掛牌在2025年12月達到17,107套——十年多以來最高。丹佛在全國撤牌排名第二。約41.9%的丹佛縣掛牌已進行價格削減。成交與報價比率軟化至98%以下。

買方市場的到來由以下跡象證明：81天在市（城市，2026年1月）vs 2021年高峰時10至15天；2025年11月成交量環比下降23%；2026年1月活躍掛牌年增8.64%。科羅拉多州房地產商協會將當前環境定性為「重新校準而非下行」。

INSURANCE & HOA: THE HIDDEN AFFORDABILITY CRISIS保險與管理費：隱藏的可負擔性危機

Colorado's insurance crisis is the most acute of any landlocked U.S. state. The average Colorado homeowner insurance premium reached approximately $4,100/year by 2025 — a 137% increase over the preceding decade, and approximately 2.7x the national average of $1,500. The drivers are Colorado-specific: the state has the highest frequency of severe hail events of any U.S. state, combined with significant wildfire risk in the Front Range urban-wildland interface.

For Denver proper, the practical impact is most visible in the condo/townhome market. HOA fees surged 20–35% in 2024–2025. A buyer qualifying for a $395,000 condo (metro median) at 6.38% (20% down) faces a monthly P&I of approximately $1,970. Adding HOA fees of $400–$700/month, the total monthly housing payment reaches $2,500–$2,800 — a front-end DTI of 37–42% for a $80,000 income household. This explains the 8% condo sales decline and 3.7% median price decline.

科羅拉多州的保險危機是任何內陸美國州中最嚴峻的。平均房主保險費達到約4,100美元/年——過去十年漲幅137%，約為全國平均1,500美元的2.7倍。驅動因素具有科羅拉多州特殊性：最高頻率嚴重冰雹事件加上前山脊顯著野火風險。

對丹佛本市而言，實際影響在公寓/聯排別墅市場中最為明顯。HOA費在2024至2025年激增20至35%。以6.38%購買395,000美元公寓，月供加HOA，總月供達2,500至2,800美元——對年收入80,000美元的家庭負債收入比37至42%。這解釋了公寓銷量8%的下降和中位數3.7%的跌幅。

SINGLE-FAMILY VS CONDO: A MARKET SPLITTING IN TWO獨棟 VS 公寓：一分為二的市場

Denver's 2025–2026 market is defined by the widening divergence between the SF and attached housing sectors. DMAR year-end data confirms: SF sales rose 3% YoY while median prices held flat at approximately $624,990. Condo/townhome sales fell 8% YoY with median prices declining 3.7% to $395,000. The divergence is driven by insurance cost escalation hitting condos disproportionately via HOA pass-through, stronger lock-in effect for SF homeowners, and entry-level buyers facing the most acute affordability headwinds.

Within the SF segment, Denver's mountain-adjacent neighborhoods (Washington Park, Capitol Hill, Cheesman Park) retain strong demand. Growth corridors like RiNo, West Colfax, and Central Park outperform the metro average. Outer suburbs — Aurora, Centennial — experience sluggish conditions. The luxury mountain resort segment (Telluride, Vail, Steamboat Springs) operates in an entirely separate market, posting $868 million in Telluride sales in 2025 despite broader metro softness.

丹佛2025至2026年市場由獨棟住宅和附屬住宅板塊之間不斷擴大的分化定義。DMAR年終數據確認：獨棟銷量年增3%，中位數持平於約624,990美元。公寓/聯排銷量年降8%，中位數跌至395,000美元。分化由保險成本不成比例衝擊公寓、獨棟業主更強的鎖定效應，以及入門級買家面臨最嚴峻可負擔性逆風驅動。

在獨棟住宅板塊內，丹佛山地毗鄰社區（Washington Park、Capitol Hill）保持強勁需求。RiNo、West Colfax等增長走廊表現優於都會區平均水平。外圍郊區條件停滯。豪華山地度假板塊（特盧賴德、韋爾）在獨立市場中運作，2025年特盧賴德錄得8.68億美元銷售額。

RENT CONTROL: COLORADO PREEMPTION租管：科州先占

Colorado has historically preempted local rent control, re-confirmed in a 2021 state bill. Denver cannot enact rent control or rent stabilization ordinances. Denver has debated renter protection measures but rent control itself remains legally unavailable. This creates a direct pass-through relationship between market conditions and rental prices.

Denver's rental market in 2025 reflects the moderated demand environment. Average two-bedroom rents were approximately $2,100/month, with vacancy rates at approximately 4.2%. Annual rent growth in 2025 was approximately 3–4%. For investors, the absence of rent control combined with Denver's high home prices (keeping many would-be buyers as renters) creates favorable structural fundamentals, though the insurance cost surge has compressed net yields.

科羅拉多州歷史上先占地方租管，在2021年州法案中被再次確認。丹佛不能頒布租管或租金穩定化條例。這在市場條件和租金價格之間創造了直接的傳導關係。

丹佛2025年兩居室平均租金約為2,100美元/月，空置率約4.2%。年度租金增長約3至4%。對投資者而言，缺乏租管加上丹佛高房價創造了有利的結構性基本面，儘管保險成本激增壓縮了淨收益率。

OUTLOOK 2026–2028展望 2026–2028

Denver's 2026–2028 outlook is the most balanced of the four sunbelt correctors. The base case (60% probability): metro median approximately $585,000, flat appreciation of 0–2% annually, continued buyer's market for condos, and modest SF appreciation of 2–3%. The CS Denver index advances from 322 toward 330–335 by end-2027. Colorado's diversified economy (aerospace, energy, healthcare, tech, tourism) provides a resilient employment floor.

The bull case (20% probability) requires meaningful rate relief (below 5.5%) to unlock the inventory standoff. This could produce 5–7% annual appreciation in 2026–2027. The SF market would benefit most; condo appreciation remains muted until insurance cost stabilization occurs.

The bear case (20% probability) centers on two compounding risks: sustained deterioration in Colorado's insurance market combined with a tech-sector recession reducing Denver's growing fintech/tech cluster employment. Condo median prices could decline 10–15% from current levels. The CS Denver index could fall to 295–305, returning to early 2023 trough levels. The key distinguishing feature: this bear case is driven by cost-of-ownership structure (insurance + HOA) rather than fundamental demand collapse — more amenable to policy intervention.

丹佛2026至2028年的展望是四個陽光地帶修正城市中最均衡的。基本情境（概率60%）：都會區中位數約585,000美元，每年0至2%持平升值，公寓持續買方市場，獨棟住宅溫和升值2至3%。CS丹佛指數從322推進至2027年底的330至335。科羅拉多州多元化經濟提供有韌性的就業底部。

樂觀情境（概率20%）需要利率緩解至5.5%以下以解除庫存僵局。可能產生5至7%年度升值。獨棟住宅市場受益最多。

悲觀情境（概率20%）以科州保險市場持續惡化加上科技行業衰退為中心。公寓中位數可能從當前水平下跌10至15%。CS指數可能降至295至305。關鍵區別：此悲觀情境由持有成本結構驅動——更易於政策干預。
