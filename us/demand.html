MIGRATION FLOWS遷徙流動

The pandemic-era migration boom that reshaped American housing demand has largely exhausted itself. Florida, which absorbed a net +85,000 domestic migrants in 2022, saw inflows collapse to just +6,000 in 2025 as insurance costs, property taxes, and climate risk repriced the Sunbelt value proposition. Austin, the poster child of tech-driven migration, plateaued at +15,000 net domestic arrivals versus its 2022 peak of +52,000. Meanwhile, the traditional outflow cities — New York, San Francisco, and Los Angeles — have seen their net losses decelerate sharply: NYC's net outflow narrowed from -45,000 to -20,000, and San Francisco has nearly reached equilibrium at -5,000.

The implication for housing demand is a normalization of geographic pressure. The Sunbelt markets that benefited from migration-driven price spikes now face a demand vacuum just as their supply pipelines deliver peak completions. Coastal gateway cities, by contrast, are experiencing a modest demand recovery as return-to-office mandates and urban amenity preferences pull population back.

疫情時期重塑美國住房需求的遷徙潮已基本耗盡。佛羅里達州在2022年吸收了+85,000的國內淨遷入人口，但到2025年隨著保險成本、房產稅及氣候風險重新定價陽光帶的價值主張，流入量驟降至僅+6,000。奧斯丁這一科技驅動遷徙的典型城市，從2022年高峰的+52,000降至+15,000的淨流入。與此同時，傳統外流城市——紐約、舊金山和洛杉磯——的淨流失大幅減緩：紐約的淨流出從-45,000收窄至-20,000，舊金山接近平衡僅為-5,000。

對住房需求的含義是地理壓力的正常化。曾受遷徙驅動價格飆升的陽光帶市場，如今在在建供給交付高峰之際面臨需求真空。相比之下，沿海門戶城市正經歷溫和的需求復甦，因為回辦公室政策和城市生活偏好將人口拉回。

EMPLOYMENT ANCHORS就業錨定

Housing demand durability correlates strongly with the diversification of the local employment base. Cities anchored by a single industry — notably San Francisco's extreme tech dependence — are vulnerable to cyclical demand swings. By contrast, markets like San Diego and Boston benefit from multi-sector employment across defense, biotech, healthcare, and technology, creating a more resilient demand floor. New York's dominance in finance provides a stable but highly compensated demand base that supports premium price levels even during periods of broader market stress.

住房需求的持久性與當地就業基礎的多元化程度高度相關。依賴單一產業的城市——尤其是舊金山對科技業的極度依賴——容易受到週期性需求波動的衝擊。相比之下，聖地牙哥和波士頓受益於國防、生物科技、醫療保健和科技等多行業就業，形成了更具韌性的需求底部。紐約在金融領域的主導地位提供了穩定且高薪的需求基礎，即使在更廣泛的市場壓力期間也能支撐溢價水平。

AFFORDABILITY可負擔性

The price-to-income (PTI) ratio remains the single most important demand constraint across U.S. markets. At a national level, the median home now costs approximately 6.0× median household income, versus the long-run historical average of 3.5–4.0×. But the city-level variation is extreme: Austin at 3.5× has corrected back toward historical norms following its supply surge, while Silicon Valley at 19.5× occupies a different economic universe entirely, sustained only by the extraordinary compensation packages of the tech sector.

Markets above the 12× threshold — San Francisco, Silicon Valley, and increasingly New York — function as cash-buyer and high-income markets where mortgage affordability is largely irrelevant. Markets below 6× retain genuine first-time buyer accessibility. The intermediate zone (6–12×) is where mortgage rate sensitivity is highest: these markets saw the sharpest volume declines when rates rose and will see the fastest recovery when rates eventually fall.

房價收入比（PTI）仍然是美國各市場最重要的需求約束。全國層面，中位數房屋價格約為中位數家庭收入的6.0倍，而長期歷史平均為3.5至4.0倍。但城市層級的差異極為懸殊：奧斯丁在供給激增後已回調至3.5倍接近歷史常態，而矽谷以19.5倍完全處於另一個經濟維度，僅靠科技業的超額薪酬維持。

超過12倍門檻的市場——舊金山、矽谷及日益接近的紐約——實質上已成為現金買家和高收入市場，按揭可負擔性基本無關緊要。低於6倍的市場仍保有真正的首次購房者可及性。中間地帶（6至12倍）是按揭利率敏感度最高的區域：這些市場在利率上升時成交量下滑最劇烈，利率最終下降時恢復也最快。

CASH BUYERS現金買家

The high-rate environment has turbocharged cash buyers' competitive advantage. Nationally, all-cash transactions now represent 28% of home purchases, up from a pre-pandemic norm of 20–22%. In luxury segments and select coastal markets, cash dominance is far more extreme: 68% of sales in San Diego's coastal submarkets and San Francisco's luxury tier close without mortgage financing. New York City's co-op and condo market sees 63% cash transactions, driven by both domestic wealth and international capital flows.

This cash buyer concentration creates a two-tier demand structure: price-insensitive buyers sustain valuations at the top while rate-sensitive first-time and move-up buyers are effectively locked out. The structural consequence is that price corrections in these markets require either a wealth shock (equity market crash) or a policy intervention (mansion tax, foreign buyer restrictions), not simply higher mortgage rates.

高利率環境極大增強了現金買家的競爭優勢。全國範圍內，全現金交易目前佔購房量的28%，高於疫情前20至22%的常態。在豪宅市場和部分沿海市場，現金主導程度更為極端：聖地牙哥沿海區域和舊金山豪宅市場有68%的成交不使用按揭融資。紐約市合作公寓及共管公寓市場63%為現金交易，受國內財富和國際資本流動共同驅動。

現金買家的集中造就了雙層需求結構：價格不敏感的買家在頂部維持估值水平，而利率敏感的首次購房者和改善型買家則被有效鎖定在外。結構性後果是，這些市場的價格修正需要財富衝擊（股市崩盤）或政策干預（豪宅稅、外國買家限制），而非僅僅是更高的按揭利率。
