CS INDEX HISTORYCASE-SHILLER 指數歷史

The S&P/Case-Shiller Home Price Index remains the gold standard for tracking U.S. residential property values. The chart below traces 10 major metros from 2015 to 2025. San Diego and Miami have seen the most dramatic appreciation over the decade, while Chicago and Phoenix started from lower bases. Notably, the pandemic boom (2020–2022) pushed nearly every market to new highs, though the subsequent rate shock produced uneven corrections. Cities with strong tech or finance employment bases — San Francisco, Seattle — experienced sharper drawdowns before partially recovering.

S&P/Case-Shiller 房價指數仍是追蹤美國住宅房價的金標準。以下圖表追蹤了2015至2025年十大都會區的走勢。聖地牙哥和邁阿密在十年間漲幅最為顯著，而芝加哥和鳳凰城起點較低。疫情繁榮期（2020至2022年）幾乎將所有市場推向新高，但後續的利率衝擊導致不均勻的回調。擁有強大科技或金融就業基礎的城市——舊金山、西雅圖——經歷了更劇烈的回撤後才部分恢復。

PRICE-TO-INCOME ANALYSIS房價收入比分析

Price-to-income (PTI) ratios measure the number of years of median household income required to purchase a median-priced home. A PTI above 6.0 is generally considered unaffordable by international standards. San Francisco and New York consistently exceed this threshold, while Chicago and Austin have returned to affordable territory following post-pandemic corrections. Austin’s PTI fell from a peak of 7.2x in 2022 to 3.5x by 2025, reflecting a rapid supply response from new construction.

房價收入比（PTI）衡量購買一套中位數價格住宅所需的家庭年收入年數。按國際標準，PTI超過6.0通常被視為不可負擔。舊金山和紐約持續超過這一門檻，而芝加哥和奧斯丁在疫情後回調中已恢復至可負擔區間。奧斯丁的PTI從2022年高峰的7.2倍降至2025年的3.5倍，反映了新建住宅的快速供給回應。

PRICE-TO-RENT RATIO租售比

The price-to-rent (PTR) ratio estimates how many years of gross rent would equal the purchase price. A PTR above 25 generally favors renting over buying. Coastal markets — San Francisco (44), New York (42), Los Angeles (38) — are deep into “rent territory,” meaning investors face poor yields relative to purchase costs. Chicago (16) and Miami (18) remain the most favorable markets for buy-to-rent investors, offering reasonable yields that justify acquisition at current price levels.

租售比（PTR）估算多少年的毛租金等於購買價格。PTR超過25通常意味著租房優於買房。沿海市場——舊金山（44）、紐約（42）、洛杉磯（38）——已深入「租房區」，表示投資者相對於購買成本面臨較差的收益率。芝加哥（16）和邁阿密（18）仍是最有利於租賃投資的市場，提供合理的收益率以證明在當前價格水準的購入合理性。

GLOBAL CONTEXT全球比較

Compared to major global gateway cities, U.S. metros offer relatively reasonable price-to-income ratios. Hong Kong remains the world’s least affordable market with a PTI of 22x, followed by Singapore at 18x. San Diego (11.5x) is the most expensive U.S. city on this measure but would rank in the middle tier globally. The median price in USD terms shows U.S. markets ranging from $720K (Tokyo) to $1.5M (Hong Kong/Singapore), with most U.S. cities clustering around the $850K–$1M range.

與主要全球門戶城市相比，美國都會區的房價收入比相對合理。香港仍是全球最不可負擔的市場，PTI達22倍，其次是新加坡18倍。聖地牙哥（11.5倍）是美國最貴的城市，但在全球排名中僅屬中等水準。以美元計的中位數價格顯示，美國市場介於72萬美元（東京）至150萬美元（香港/新加坡）之間，大多數美國城市集中在85萬至100萬美元區間。
