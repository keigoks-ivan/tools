EXECUTIVE SUMMARY執行摘要

San Francisco's housing market in 2026 is the most dramatic reversal story in this monitor. After experiencing the deepest pandemic-era downtown collapse of any major U.S. metro — office vacancy reaching 37%, population declining by 60,000, and the CS index falling from a peak of 383 (May 2022) to approximately 340 by early 2023 — the city has staged a recovery so rapid that Redfin named San Francisco and San Jose the two fastest-selling home markets in the United States as of September 2025. The catalyst is artificial intelligence. OpenAI, Anthropic, Salesforce's Einstein AI division, Google DeepMind, and hundreds of AI startups have flooded the Bay Area with high-compensated talent. SF posted median single-family prices of $1,653,325 in January 2026 — up 16.23% YoY — with homes selling in a median of just 13 days and 15% above asking price.

The recovery is bifurcated. Single-family homes have powered decisively into new territory, with Q4 2025 median of approximately $1.8M matching the June 2022 peak. The condo market is staging a more tentative recovery. The January 2026 condo median of $1,020,000 (+2.77% YoY) represents stabilization rather than surge — still materially below the 2022 peak of approximately $1.2–1.3M.

San Francisco's supply situation is the most extreme of any market in this monitor. The January 2026 active SF home inventory of 148 units represents a 37.82% YoY decline — the only county in the Bay Area to not record an annual increase. With 0.8 months of supply, the theoretical clearing time is less than one month. The structural drivers: geographic confinement (49-square-mile peninsula), Proposition 13's property tax lock-in, and zoning restrictions that have historically limited new construction to a fraction of demand.

2026年舊金山住宅市場是本監測中最戲劇性的逆轉故事。在經歷最深的疫情時代市中心崩潰——辦公室空置率達37%、人口減少60,000人、CS指數從383的峰值下跌至2023年初的約340——之後，城市的復甦速度之快使Redfin在2025年9月將舊金山和聖荷西評為美國兩個成交最快的住宅市場。催化劑是人工智能。OpenAI、Anthropic、Salesforce的Einstein AI部門、谷歌DeepMind和數百家AI初創公司以高薪人才充斥灣區。2026年1月舊金山市錄得1,653,325美元的獨棟住宅中位數——年增16.23%——房屋中位成交時間僅13天，高於要求報價15%。

復甦呈分化。獨棟住宅已有力推進至新領域，2025年第四季約180萬美元的中位數與2022年6月峰值持平。公寓市場進行更試探性的復甦。2026年1月102萬美元的公寓中位數（年增2.77%）代表穩定而非激增——仍明顯低於2022年峰值約120至130萬美元。

舊金山的供給狀況是本監測任何市場中最極端的。2026年1月148套的活躍獨棟住宅庫存代表年降37.82%——灣區唯一未錄得年度庫存增加的縣。以0.8個月的供給，理論出清時間不足一個月。結構性驅動因素：地理圍合（49平方英里半島）、第13號提案財產稅鎖定，以及歷史上將新建住宅限制在需求一小部分的分區限制。

PRICE TREND: TROUGH, RECOVERY & AI-FUELLED SURGE房價走勢：谷底、復甦與AI驅動激增

San Francisco's price trajectory is defined by three distinct phases. The pre-pandemic tech boom (2015–2019) drove the CS SF index from approximately 270 to 360 — a 33% increase. The pandemic phase (2020–2022) produced the city's final euphoric peak: the CS index reached 383 by May 2022. The correction phase (2022–2023) was the most severe on the coasts — the CS index fell from 383 to approximately 340–350, a peak-to-trough of approximately 10–12%. The recovery phase (2024–2026) is now clearly established as AI-driven. The CS index recovered to 364 by February 2025 and city data shows Q4 2025 SF median at $1.8M — virtually matching the May 2022 all-time high.

舊金山的房價走勢由三個截然不同的階段定義。疫情前科技繁榮（2015至2019年）將CS舊金山指數從約270推至360——漲幅33%。疫情階段（2020至2022年）產生城市最後的欣快峰值：CS指數在2022年5月達到383。修正階段（2022至2023年）是所有沿海市場中最嚴峻的——CS指數從383降至約340至350，峰值至谷值約10至12%。復甦階段（2024至2026年）現在已清楚確立為AI驅動。CS指數到2025年2月恢復至364，城市數據顯示2025年Q4獨棟中位數為$180萬——實際上與2022年5月的歷史最高點持平。

THE AI ECONOMY: NEW DEMAND REPLACING THE LOST TECH DECADEAI經濟：取代失落科技十年的新需求

The AI industry's emergence as San Francisco's dominant economic driver represents a structural demand replacement. The 2022–2023 narrative was one of irreversible urban exodus: office vacancy reached 37%. The conventional wisdom was that San Francisco's moment had passed. The AI moment inverted this thesis within 18 months. OpenAI (Mission District), Anthropic (SoMa), Scale AI, xAI, and hundreds of AI-focused startups have concentrated in the SoMa/Mission Bay/Mission District corridor. AI engineers command $300,000–$600,000 total compensation packages. The September 2025 pending sales surge of 17% YoY is directly attributable to this buyer cohort.

AI行業作為舊金山主導經濟驅動力的出現代表了結構性需求替代。2022至2023年的敘事是不可逆的城市外逃：辦公室空置率達到37%。AI時刻在18個月內逆轉了這一論點。OpenAI（Mission District）、Anthropic（SoMa）、Scale AI、xAI和數百家AI初創公司集中在SoMa/Mission Bay/Mission District走廊。AI工程師獲得30萬至60萬美元的總薪酬方案。2025年9月17%的待成交量漲幅直接可歸因於這一買家群體。

THE CONDO RECOVERY: FROM DEEP DISCOUNT TO STABILIZATION公寓復甦：從深度折扣到穩定

The SF condo market's pandemic correction was among the most severe in this monitor. From a peak median of approximately $1,250,000 (2022), the SF condo market declined to approximately $900,000–$950,000 by mid-2023 — a 25–28% nominal decline. The recovery to January 2026 median of $1,020,000 (+2.77% YoY) represents stabilization rather than a return to peak. Buildings with strong reserves, recent renovations, and proximity to transit are outperforming, while aging downtown-dependent buildings continue to face buyer resistance.

舊金山公寓市場的疫情修正是本監測中最嚴峻的之一。從約125萬美元的峰值中位數（2022年），到2023年中降至約90至95萬美元——名義下跌25至28%。恢復至2026年1月102萬美元的中位數（年增2.77%）代表穩定而非回到峰值。儲備充足、近期翻新且靠近交通的建築表現優於該類別。

SUPPLY CONSTRAINT: GEOGRAPHY, PROP 13 & ZONING供給約束：地理、第13號提案與分區

San Francisco's supply constraint is structural, multi-layered, and essentially permanent. The geographic reality: a 49-square-mile peninsula surrounded by the Pacific Ocean, San Francisco Bay, and adjacent counties — no land to expand into. Proposition 13 (1978) creates a powerful lock-in: a homeowner who bought in 1980 for $200,000 pays approximately $3,500/year in property taxes; selling for a $1.5M replacement triggers approximately $18,750/year. This lock-in has removed hundreds of thousands of potential listings from SF's annual supply.

In January 2026, there were 148 SF homes for sale — the lowest in years — against a city of 875,000 residents plus 300,000+ daily commuters. The SF market operates at essentially zero functional supply: properties are not marketed, they are auctioned.

舊金山的供給約束是結構性的、多層次的、本質上永久的。地理現實：49平方英里的半島被太平洋、舊金山灣和鄰縣包圍——無地可擴。第13號提案（1978年）創造了強大的鎖定效應：1980年以20萬美元購買的業主年付約3,500美元財產稅；出售以150萬美元替換物業則觸發年付約18,750美元。

2026年1月，舊金山有148套獨棟住宅待售——多年來最低——面對875,000居民加30萬以上日常通勤者。獨棟住宅市場以幾乎零功能供給運作：房產不是被推廣，而是被拍賣。

RENT CONTROL: SAN FRANCISCO’S COMPREHENSIVE FRAMEWORK租管：舊金山的全面框架

San Francisco has one of the most comprehensive rent control frameworks of any U.S. city, governed by the San Francisco Rent Ordinance (1979). It applies to residential units built before June 13, 1979 (approximately 172,000 units). Annual increases are capped at local CPI (typically 1–3%). Just-cause eviction requirements prevent landlords from removing long-term tenants without qualifying reasons. California's Costa-Hawkins Act (1995) exempts SFRs, condos, and units built after 1995 from local rent control.

This creates two distinct investment profiles: (1) pre-1979 multi-unit buildings with rent control where increasing rent requires tenant turnover (vacancy decontrol); (2) post-1995 condos/SFRs with no rent control. Long-term tenants in controlled units often pay 50–70% of market rent — some pay $1,200–$1,800/month in a market where 1BR averages $3,125/month.

舊金山擁有美國任何城市中最全面的租管框架，由1979年《舊金山租金條例》規範。適用於1979年6月13日前建造的住宅單位（約172,000套）。年度漲租上限為當地CPI（通常1至3%）。正當原因驅逐要求阻止房東在沒有合格理由下驅逐長期租戶。加州1995年《科斯塔-霍金斯法》免除獨棟住宅、公寓和1995年後建造的單位不受地方租管限制。

這創造了兩種截然不同的投資模型：（1）受租管的1979年前多單位建築（需要租戶換租才能提高租金）；（2）不受租管的1995年後公寓/獨棟住宅。管制單位的長期租戶往往支付市場租金的50至70%——部分人支付$1,200至$1,800/月，而市場一居室均值為$3,125/月。

OUTLOOK 2026–2028展望 2026–2028

San Francisco's outlook is the most momentum-driven of any market in this monitor. The base case (55% probability) projects CS SF appreciation of 5–8% annually through 2027, driven by sustained AI hiring, multiple AI IPOs in 2026 generating RSU liquidity for homebuying, and continued office recovery. The CS index advances from 364 toward 400–415 by end-2027. SF home prices could approach $2.0M median by end-2026.

The bull case (25% probability) is an AI IPO supercycle. If multiple $50B+ company IPOs materialize in 2026–2027, the RSU liquidity event would be the largest single-year wealth creation since the 2000 dot-com peak. This could drive 15–25% appreciation in a compressed 12-month window.

The bear case (20% probability) is an AI investment bubble correction. If AI revenue projections prove difficult to monetize, demand reverses with the same speed it arrived. The CS index could retrace to 330–340 (2023 trough), SF home medians fall 15–20%, and the condo market re-tests 2023 lows. The key risk: AI demand is more concentrated in a single industry than any prior SF tech cycle, making the potential correction sharper but potentially shorter.

舊金山的展望是本監測任何市場中動能最強的。基本情境（概率55%）預計CS舊金山每年升值5至8%，CS指數推進至2027年底的400至415。獨棟住宅價格到2026年底可能接近200萬美元中位數。

樂觀情境（概率25%）是AI IPO超週期。如果多家$500億以上公司IPO在2026至2027年實現，可能在12個月窗口內推動15至25%的升值。

悲觀情境（概率20%）是AI投資泡沫修正。如果AI收入預測難以貨幣化，需求以同樣速度逆轉。CS指數可能回撤至330至340（2023年谷底），獨棟中位數下跌15至20%。關鍵風險：AI需求比任何以往科技週期更集中在單一行業，使修正更尖銳但可能更短。
