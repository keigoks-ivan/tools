EXECUTIVE SUMMARY執行摘要

Seattle's housing market in 2026 presents the clearest expression of the supply-constrained tech-employment model among all U.S. markets in this monitor. The Seattle-Tacoma-Bellevue MSA's combination of geographic confinement (Puget Sound to the west, Cascade Mountains to the east, tight city limits between), zoning restrictions, and a uniquely high concentration of the highest-paid tech workers in the world — Amazon alone employs 50,000+ in Seattle — has produced a market where median home prices remain at $780,000–$848,000 despite the most severe rate cycle in 40 years. The Case-Shiller Seattle index (SEXRSA) peaked at 414 in May 2022 and had corrected only to 391 by February 2025 — a peak-to-trough of 5.6%, the shallowest correction of any West Coast market. Phoenix corrected 30%+, Austin 25%, Denver 20%; Seattle, 5.6%.

The shallow correction is no accident — it reflects a structural supply floor that prevents inventory from clearing at lower prices. Seattle's geography physically limits outward expansion: the city is bounded by water on three sides and mountains on a fourth, and King County zoning has historically restricted infill density. New single-family permits averaged approximately 500/month in 2024–2025, far below the demand implied by population growth. Against this supply backdrop, even the significant demand reduction caused by rate lock-in and tech-sector layoffs has not produced the price clearing that affordability math would suggest. December 2025 inventory reached 6,356 active listings — the highest winter level since 2019, but still a fraction of the 12,000–15,000 listings typical of balanced pre-Amazon markets.

The most consequential new development for Seattle's rental and investment market is Washington State's HB 1217, which took effect May 7, 2025 — making Washington the third U.S. state to adopt statewide rent stabilization. HB 1217 caps annual rent increases for existing tenants at 7% plus local CPI, or 10%, whichever is lower. Seattle City Council has additionally banned the use of algorithmic rent-setting software — a direct response to the RealPage/Department of Justice antitrust case. For investors, this represents a structural change from Washington's previously landlord-favorable regulatory environment.

2026年西雅圖住宅市場在本監測所有美國市場中最清晰地體現了供給受限的科技就業模型。西雅圖-塔科馬-貝爾維尤都會區的地理圍合（西臨普吉特灣、東臨喀斯喀特山脈、城市邊界緊湊）、分區限制，以及全球最高薪科技工作者的獨特高度集中——僅亞馬遜在西雅圖就僱用50,000名以上工人——造就了一個即使在40年來最嚴峻的利率週期中，房屋中位數仍維持在780,000至848,000美元的市場。Case-Shiller西雅圖指數（SEXRSA）在2022年5月達到414的峰值，到2025年2月僅修正至391——峰值至谷值5.6%，是所有西海岸市場中最淺的修正。鳳凰城修正30%以上、奧斯丁25%、丹佛20%；西雅圖，5.6%。

這一淺度修正並非偶然——它反映了阻止庫存在較低價格出清的結構性供給底部。西雅圖的地理條件從物理上限制了向外擴張：城市三面環水、一面靠山，金縣分區法歷史上限制了城市填充密度。2024至2025年新獨棟住宅許可月均約500件，遠低於人口增長所隱含的需求。在此供給背景下，即便是利率鎖定和科技行業裁員造成的顯著需求減少，也未能產生可負擔性算術所預示的價格出清。2025年12月庫存達到6,356套活躍掛牌——2019年以來最高冬季水平，但仍遠少於均衡的前亞馬遜市場典型的12,000至15,000套掛牌。

西雅圖租賃和投資市場最重要的新發展是華盛頓州HB 1217，於2025年5月7日生效——使華盛頓州成為美國第三個採用州級租金穩定化的州。HB 1217將現有租戶的年度漲租上限設為當地CPI加7%或10%，取低者。西雅圖市議會還額外禁止使用算法租金設定軟件——這是對RealPage/司法部反壟斷案的直接回應。對投資者而言，這代表華盛頓州從之前有利於房東的監管環境發生的結構性變化。

PRICE TREND: PEAK, CORRECTION & STABILIZATION房價走勢：峰值、修正與穩定

Seattle's price history over the decade 2015–2025 divides into three distinct regimes. The Amazon Effect era (2015–2018) saw the most rapid price appreciation of any major U.S. city, as Amazon's headcount in Seattle grew from approximately 25,000 to 50,000 and the secondary economic multiplier drew waves of ancillary tech workers. The CS Seattle index appreciated from approximately 230 to 335 over this period — 46% in four years. The 2018–2019 correction was triggered by Amazon's New York HQ2 announcement and preceded the pandemic by 18 months; prices moderated 5–8% before rebounding. The pandemic surge (2020–2022) accelerated to a CS peak of 414 and a city median above $900,000 before the rate shock produced the shallowest correction in the West Coast peer group.

Current data shows the market stabilizing well above long-run trend: the Zillow home value index for Seattle city stands at $848,000 as of February 2026 (down 1.7% YoY), while the December 2025 median sale price was $780,000. The price-per-square-foot in Seattle averages approximately $550, ranging from $700–800 in Belltown and Capitol Hill to $400–500 in Renton and Rainier Valley. The ARM share of Seattle-area originations is approximately 28% — among the highest in the nation, as buyers stretch to reach affordability in a market where the 30-year fixed payment on a median home at 6.38% exceeds $4,000/month.

西雅圖2015至2025年的十年房價歷史分為三個截然不同的制度。「亞馬遜效應」時代（2015至2018年）見證了任何主要美國城市中最快速的房價升值，隨著亞馬遜在西雅圖的員工數從約25,000人增至50,000人，CS西雅圖指數從約230升至335——四年漲幅46%。2018至2019年的修正由亞馬遜紐約HQ2宣告觸發，價格溫和回調5至8%後反彈。疫情漲幅（2020至2022年）加速至CS峰值414和城市中位數900,000美元以上，隨後利率衝擊產生了西海岸同儕群體中最淺的修正。

當前數據顯示市場在遠高於長期趨勢的水平穩定：截至2026年2月，西雅圖城市Zillow房屋價值指數為848,000美元（年降1.7%），2025年12月中位數成交價為780,000美元。西雅圖每平方英尺均價約550美元。ARM佔房貸發放量約28%——全國最高之列，因為買家在中位數房屋30年固定月供以6.38%利率超過4,000美元的市場中勉強達到可負擔性。

SUPPLY & DEMAND: INVENTORY RECOVERY供需：庫存回升

Seattle's supply dynamics are among the most constrained of any U.S. market, driven by a combination of geographic limits and regulatory barriers. Single-family permits have averaged approximately 500/month since March 2023 — well below the 700–900/month of 2022 and the decade prior. King County's Comprehensive Plan debate has constrained infill density decisions. The December 2025 active listings of 6,356 for the metro represent the highest winter inventory since 2019 but remain far below what would characterize a balanced market for a metro of 4+ million.

Demand remains supported by the income profile of the buyer pool. Amazon employs 50,000+ workers in Seattle at a median compensation exceeding $200,000; Microsoft's Redmond campus employs approximately 55,000 with similar compensation profiles. Boeing employs approximately 30,000. This employer composition — concentrated in the top 5% of the U.S. income distribution — creates a buyer pool that is partially insulated from rate impacts via cash purchasing (Seattle's cash buyer share is approximately 25–30% in the $1M+ segment). The market segments sharply by income: sub-$700K properties face genuine affordability headwinds, while $1M+ properties are competed for by a pool of highly compensated cash-capable buyers who are less rate-sensitive.

西雅圖的供給動態是美國任何市場中最受限的，由地理限制和監管壁壘的組合驅動。自2023年3月以來，獨棟住宅許可月均約500件——遠低於2022年及此前十年的700至900件/月。金縣綜合計劃辯論制約了城市填充密度決策。都會區2025年12月的6,356套活躍掛牌代表2019年以來最高冬季庫存，但仍遠低於400萬以上人口都會區均衡市場的特徵水平。

需求仍由買家群體的收入構成支撐。亞馬遜在西雅圖僱用50,000名以上工人，薪酬中位數超過200,000美元；微軟雷德蒙德園區僱用約55,000人，薪酬構成相似。波音僱用約30,000人。這種雇主構成——集中在美國收入分布的前5%——創造了一個通過現金購買部分免疫利率影響的買家群體。市場按收入急劇分層：70萬美元以下房產面臨利率算術的真實可負擔性逆風，而100萬美元以上房產由高薪現金能力買家競購。

TECH EMPLOYMENT: AMAZON, MICROSOFT & CONCENTRATION RISK科技就業：亞馬遜、微軟與集中度風險

Seattle's housing market has a single-employer concentration risk that is unmatched in any other U.S. metro of comparable size. Amazon and Microsoft together directly employ approximately 105,000 workers in the greater Seattle area — accounting for an estimated 8–10% of the total employed workforce. When indirect and multiplier jobs are included, these two companies are estimated to support 20–25% of total metro employment. No major U.S. market of Seattle's size has this level of corporate concentration; the closest analogue is Detroit's historical dependence on the auto industry.

The practical risk manifested between 2022 and 2024. Amazon announced multiple rounds of layoffs totaling approximately 27,000 positions globally, with Seattle operations disproportionately affected. Microsoft conducted several rounds totaling approximately 10,000+ positions globally. These layoffs contributed to the CS index correction of 5.6% from peak. The more consequential risk is a scenario in which AI-driven productivity improvements allow Amazon or Microsoft to maintain or grow revenue with meaningfully fewer employees. If tech employment in Seattle contracts by 15–20% — a scenario within the range of AI-impact estimates for software engineering employment — the demand anchor for Seattle's high-priced market would be substantially weakened.

西雅圖住宅市場擁有同等規模任何其他美國大都市中無可比擬的單一雇主集中度風險。亞馬遜和微軟在大西雅圖地區合計直接僱用約105,000名工人——估計佔都會區總就業勞動力的8至10%。當考慮間接和乘數就業時，這兩家公司估計支撐都會區總就業的20至25%。沒有任何同等規模的主要美國市場有此水平的企業集中度；最接近的類比是底特律歷史上對汽車業的依賴。

實際風險在2022至2024年間顯現。亞馬遜宣佈多輪裁員，全球合計約27,000個職位，西雅圖業務受到不成比例的影響。微軟進行了幾輪全球合計約10,000個以上職位的裁減。這些裁員促成了CS指數較峰值5.6%的修正。更重要的風險是AI驅動的生產力提升使亞馬遜或微軟能夠以明顯更少的員工維持或增長收入。若西雅圖科技就業收縮15至20%——一個在軟件工程就業AI影響估算範圍內的情境——西雅圖高價市場的需求錨點將大幅削弱。

GEOGRAPHIC CONSTRAINT: WATER, MOUNTAINS & ZONING地理約束：水域、山脈與分區

Seattle's physical geography is the most constrained of any major U.S. West Coast city. The City of Seattle sits on a narrow isthmus between Puget Sound (to the west) and Lake Washington (to the east), with a hilly topography that limits flat, buildable land. Unlike Phoenix or Austin, where urban sprawl can absorb demand indefinitely in flat terrain, Seattle cannot build its way out of supply constraints without significant upzoning of existing residential neighborhoods.

The zoning landscape is shifting slowly. Seattle's One Seattle Comprehensive Plan (20-year framework) proposes expanded multifamily zones, increased ADU capacity, and density increases near transit stations. However, neighborhood opposition and legal challenges have historically slowed implementation. The new rent cap (HB 1217) creates additional uncertainty for multifamily investment returns, potentially slowing the apartment pipeline at exactly the moment when new supply is needed. Construction costs in Seattle average $450–600/sq ft for multifamily — making sub-$400,000 new construction economically nonviable without significant land subsidies.

西雅圖的地理條件是主要美國西海岸城市中最受限的。西雅圖市坐落於普吉特灣和華盛頓湖之間的狹窄地峽上，丘陵地形限制了平坦可建設用地。與鳳凰城或奧斯丁平坦地形可無限吸納需求不同，西雅圖在不對現有住宅社區進行大規模提高容積率的情況下，無法通過建設解決供給約束。

分區格局正在緩慢轉變。西雅圖《一個西雅圖綜合計劃》（20年框架）提議擴大多戶住宅區和交通站點附近密度。然而，社區反對和法律挑戰歷史上拖慢了實施進度。新租管上限（HB 1217）為多戶住宅投資回報創造了額外不確定性。西雅圖多戶住宅建設成本平均每平方英尺450至600美元——使40萬美元以下新建項目缺乏經濟可行性。

WA HB 1217: WASHINGTON’S NEW RENT CAPWA HB 1217：華州新租管上限

Washington State's HB 1217, signed into law and effective May 7, 2025, makes Washington the third U.S. state (after California and Oregon) to enact statewide rent stabilization. The law caps annual rent increases for existing tenants at 7% plus local CPI, or 10% — whichever is lower. Key provisions: (1) applies to residential tenancies, not commercial; (2) exempts new construction for the first 10 years; (3) does not include vacancy decontrol — landlords can reset to market on tenant turnover; (4) applies statewide, preempting any local ordinance from setting a lower cap.

For Seattle landlords, the 10% cap is relevant primarily when market conditions would otherwise support larger increases — which in Seattle's high-demand environment means the cap was binding in 2021–2022 (when market rents rose 15–20% YoY) and becomes relevant again in any future demand surge. Under normal 3–5% annual market rent growth, the cap is not binding. The Seattle City Council's additional ban on algorithmic rent-setting software (effective July 2025) responds to the national RealPage/DOJ antitrust litigation. For investors, the combination of HB 1217 and the algorithmic ban requires a shift from revenue-maximization to cash-flow-optimization strategies.

2025年5月7日生效的華盛頓州HB 1217使華盛頓成為美國第三個頒布州級租金穩定化的州。該法律將現有租戶的年度漲租上限設為當地CPI加7%或10%——取低者。關鍵條款：（1）適用於住宅租賃；（2）新建住宅在前10年豁免；（3）不包含空置解管制——房東在租戶換租時可重置為市場租金；（4）在全州適用。

對西雅圖房東而言，10%上限主要在市場條件本可支持更大漲幅時才相關——在2021至2022年（市場租金年漲15至20%）時具有約束力。在正常的年均3至5%市場租金增長下，上限沒有約束力。西雅圖市議會禁止算法租金設定軟件（2025年7月生效），回應全國RealPage/司法部反壟斷訴訟。對投資者而言，HB 1217和算法禁令的組合要求從收入最大化向現金流優化策略轉變。

OUTLOOK 2026–2028展望 2026–2028

Seattle's 2026–2028 outlook is binary in a way that no other market in this monitor is. The base case (55% probability) projects modest appreciation of 2–4% annually, driven by continued supply constraint, stable Amazon/Microsoft employment, and gradual rate relief toward 5.5–6.0%. In this scenario, the CS Seattle index advances from 391 toward 415–430 by end-2027, modestly exceeding the May 2022 all-time high.

The bull case (20% probability) requires AI to accelerate tech hiring rather than replacing it — a scenario where the AI infrastructure buildout generates enough new high-compensated engineering roles to offset efficiency-driven headcount reduction. In this scenario, Seattle's market would see strong appreciation toward 450–470 on the CS index by end-2027.

The bear case (25% probability) is uniquely Seattle-specific: AI-driven workforce reduction at Amazon and Microsoft that shrinks tech employment by 15–20%. A 15–20% contraction in the two companies' Seattle headcount would remove approximately 15,000–20,000 high-compensated buyers from the market. Absent supply flexibility (which Seattle lacks by geography), prices would decline 15–20% over 2–3 years. The CS index could fall to 315–330, a level last seen in 2018–2019 but achievable without a macroeconomic crisis if the tech-employment reduction is concentrated enough.

西雅圖2026至2028年的展望以本監測任何其他市場都不具備的方式呈現二元性。基本情境（概率55%）預計每年溫和升值2至4%，CS西雅圖指數推進至2027年底的415至430，小幅超越2022年5月的歷史高點。

樂觀情境（概率20%）需要AI加速科技招聘而非取代它——AI基礎設施建設產生足夠多的新高薪工程職位以抵消效率驅動的員工削減。CS指數到2027年底向450至470強勁升值。

悲觀情境（概率25%）是西雅圖獨有的：AI驅動的勞動力削減使亞馬遜和微軟科技就業收縮15至20%。兩家公司西雅圖員工數收縮15至20%將從市場中移除約15,000至20,000名高薪買家。在沒有供給彈性的情況下，價格將在2至3年內下跌15至20%。CS指數可能降至315至330。
